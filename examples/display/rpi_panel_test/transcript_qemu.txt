==== visual verification (the other half of "lit") ====
The gate runs headless (-display none), so the checksums above are re-run with a
QEMU monitor attached and the PANEL console dumped after the fill:

  qemu-system-arm -M mimxrt1170-evk -global fsl-imxrt1170.boot-xip=on \
      -kernel build/rpi_panel_test.elf -display none \
      -serial file:... -monitor unix:mon.sock,server,nowait
  (qemu) screendump panel.ppm

  panel.ppm: P6, 800 x 480, maxval 255, 1152000 pixel bytes
  distinct colours: 1
  RGB = (248, 0, 0) = #F80000, count 384000 (every pixel)

800x480 is the LCDIFv2 layer-0 geometry (the model resizes the console to it),
and #F80000 is exactly RGB565 0xF800 as the LCDIFv2 model's RGB565 draw line
renders it: it zero-fills the low bits (31 << 3 = 248) rather than replicating
them, which is the RENDERER's 565->console-surface expansion and is unrelated to
the 565->888->565 fill seam this gate checksums.  (Not separately observed here:
the same model refuses to paint the console at all until the bridge reports
downstream_ready, so these 384000 pixels also mean the bring-up was accepted.)
